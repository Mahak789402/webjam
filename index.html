A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

(1931- 2015)

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was an eminent Indian scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). He is widely respected for his humility, intelligence, and contribution to India's scientific and defense advancements.

🌟 Quick Facts:

  • Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
  • Born: October 15, 1931 – Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • Died: July 27, 2015 – Shillong, Meghalaya (while delivering a lecture)
  • Nickname: Missile Man of India
  • Profession: Aerospace Scientist, Author, Teacher, Former Presi
  • Presidency: 2002–2007 (People’s President)

🧪 Scientific Career Highlights:

  • Worked with ISRO and DRDO
  • Key figure in India's missile development, including: Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Played a leading role in India’s 1998 nuclear tests at Pokhran.
  • Major contributor to India’s satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) project

📚 Popular Books Written by Abdul Kalam:

  • Wings of Fire – His autobiography.
  • Ignited Minds – Motivational book for young Indians.
  • India 2020 – A vision for transforming India into a developed nation.
  • My Journey – Personal stories from his life.

❤️ Legacy and Influence:

  • Known for his love for students and youth empowerment.
  • Believed in a visionary India led by innovation, education, and hard work.
  • Even after his presidency, he continued to teach and inspire.
  • "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action." – one of his famous quotes.

🕊️ Death:

  • He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.
  • His sudden death deeply shocked and saddened the nation.
